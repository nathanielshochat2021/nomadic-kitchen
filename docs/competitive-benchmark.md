## The landscape

Nomadic Kitchen sits between four adjacent categories. We are **none of them**,
which is the point.

| Category | Examples | Typical price | Why we're different |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Built-in masonry outdoor kitchens** | Local masons, BBQ islands | $15k–$60k+ | We're mobile, hand-built in wood, and a fraction of the cost. No construction project. |
| **Modular metal outdoor kitchen carts** | Big-box & premium grill brands | $2k–$12k | We're solid wood and built-to-order, not flat-pack metal. Editorial, heirloom feel. |
| **Overlanding / chuck-box camp kitchens** | Trailer & roof-rack kitchens | $1k–$8k | We're a full kitchen, not a fold-out box — and equally at home on a patio. |
| **Food carts / concession trailers** | Vending trailers | $5k–$50k | We're consumer lifestyle, not commercial vending. |

## Where we win

- **Material & craft.** Solid wood, joined by hand. Nobody in the cart space
  leads with this.
- **One configurable product.** Easy to understand, easy to upsell — vs.
  sprawling SKUs or bespoke quotes with no transparency.
- **Dual use.** Patio on casters *or* trailer for the trail. Few products serve
  both audiences credibly.
- **Brand.** Premium, editorial, photography-led. The category is mostly utility
  marketing.

## Where we must be careful

- **Price perception** vs. flat-pack metal carts — lead with craft, wood,
  made-to-order and longevity, not spec-sheet parity.
- **Lead time** vs. in-stock products — frame the deposit/build model as a
  feature (it's *yours*), not a delay.
- **Weather durability** messaging — be explicit about finishes and covers.

## Positioning statement

> For homeowners and adventurers who want a real outdoor kitchen with soul,
> Nomadic Kitchen is the hand-built, wood-clad, made-to-order cart that goes
> wherever you do — unlike flat-pack metal carts or fixed masonry builds.
